Primary Goal

We provide various request forms, including Business Permits, Barangay Permits, Certificates of Indigency, Good Moral Character, Jobseeker Assistance, Barangay Clearance, and Barangay ID to support your personal and professional requirements.

Types of Papers

Certificates

A barangay certificate is an official document issued by the barangay, which is the smallest administrative unit in the Philippines, certifying a particular fact, status, or relationship concerning an individual within the community. Common types of barangay certificates include a barangay clearance, which confirms an individual's good standing with no pending legal issues, and a barangay certificate of residency, verifying that a person resides within a specific barangay. Other types include the barangay certificate of indigency, which certifies a person as low-income or indigent, and the barangay certificate of good moral character, confirming that an individual is of good moral standing. These certificates are often required for various purposes, such as job applications, government services, legal matters, and social welfare benefits. They are typically issued by the barangay secretary or an authorized official and may require supporting documents such as proof of identity and residence.

Permits

A barangay permit is a legal authorization issued by a barangay (the smallest administrative unit in the Philippines) that allows for particular activities or economic operations within its jurisdiction. It is frequently necessary for a variety of objectives, including as establishing a business, hosting specific events, or engaging in activities that may have an impact on the local community. For example, persons or entities intending to operate a business inside the barangay's boundaries must get barangay business permits. This guarantees that the business follows municipal regulations, zoning laws, and community principles. Similarly, licenses for community events like as gatherings, fiestas, or public activities may be provided by the barangay to guarantee that they are carried out in an orderly and lawful way. Barangay permits frequently require the submission of application forms, the provision of essential documentation (such as business registration, proof of domicile, or event details), and the payment of a charge. These permits allow the barangay to monitor and regulate activities inside its boundaries, assuring the safety, order, and welfare of its citizens.

First time Job Seekers

A First Time Job Seeker Certificate is a certificate provided by a barangay in the Philippines to individuals seeking employment for the first time. This certificate verifies that the applicant has never worked before and is frequently necessary to obtain benefits under the First Time Job Seekers Act (Republic Act No. 11261). The law offers incentives such as fee exemptions or reductions for government documents like as NBI clearances, police clearances, barangay clearances, and PhilHealth registration, all of which are normally required for employment. To receive the certificate, candidates must go to the barangay hall and present proof of identity (a valid ID or birth certificate), proof of non-employment (a school diploma or an affidavit), and any required application forms. The certificate enables first-time job seekers to access government assistance and reduces the costs of obtaining required paperwork for job applications. It also ensures the safety, order, and welfare of its citizens.
